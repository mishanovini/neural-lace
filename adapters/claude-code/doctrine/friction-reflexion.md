# Friction-reflexion — compact
> Enforcement: Pattern — self-applied
> Applies: when friction surfaces in the natural course of assigned work

Notice friction AS it arises — a gate fires unexpectedly, a tool lacks a clean
path, a process produces a sub-optimal outcome, you catch yourself mid-mistake,
your own approach is less efficient than it could be. Do not go hunting for
improvements; notice what the work itself surfaces.

**Surface immediately, never silently.** In the same turn, as a short plain-text
passage — one or two sentences naming the friction plus a proposed fix, framed
explicitly as a suggestion for discussion. Do not fold it into a session-end
summary or defer it to "later."

**Suggestion only — never unilateral action.** Until the operator has discussed
the suggestion and explicitly approved a next step, do NOT file it to a
backlog, create a plan, edit a rule/hook/agent, or take any action that converts
the suggestion into committed work. Propose; the operator decides.

**Self-throttle.** Monitor your own friction-surfacing for noise. After
several low-value suggestions in a row, stop and reserve surfacing for real
signal — a stream of marginal interruptions is itself friction you're imposing.

Applies to every agent, including the orchestrator — it is best positioned to
notice process-level friction (stale tasks, gates firing across builders,
inefficient dispatch) and is explicitly in scope.

This does not relax bug-persistence for observed product bugs (doctrine
persistence rule, constitution §5) — that is a different obligation. It does
not license narrate-and-wait — surface the friction and keep working.
