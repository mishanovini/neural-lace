# Teaching moments — compact
> Enforcement: Pattern — self-applied (opt-in per project via docs/teaching-examples/)
> Applies: when the operator's pushback shifts your position to something meaningfully better

Capture the moment when ALL of these hold: the operator pushed back
substantively on your position (not a typo fix, a real challenge to your
reasoning); your revised position is meaningfully better; the lesson
generalizes to other developers on other projects; enough context survives for
a reader who wasn't there.

The bar is high — a typical session produces zero; a high-signal session
produces one. Don't capture domain knowledge the operator simply supplied, or
self-corrections with no operator prompt behind them.

Write to `docs/teaching-examples/YYYY-MM-DD-<slug>.md`: frontmatter (date,
topic, lesson, project, participants, related-files) then Context / Claude's
initial position / the operator's pushback / the corrected position / the
lesson for developers (2-4 bullets) / optional generalization.

Prioritize brevity (under 5 minutes to read) and transferability (strip
project-specific names where the lesson is general). Capture the failure
honestly — don't soften "Claude was wrong" into "we explored different
framings."
